Research

Primary Author · Abstract Accepted · ICPACT 2026
Dual-Modality Interpretable AI Framework
for Breast Cancer Triage
Amity University Dubai · In Progress

As primary author, I led the research design, literature review, and end-to-end development of the FNA cytology pipeline for this dual-modality breast cancer detection system. The work centers on building clinically interpretable ML models that are transparent, statistically rigorous, and suitable for real-world diagnostic triage.

Preprocessing

Iterative imputation for missing values, RobustScaler for outlier management, stratified 60/20/20 train-test splits.

Model Evaluation

Comparative evaluation across 7 supervised classifiers on 569-sample FNA dataset using sensitivity, specificity, AUROC, and cross-validation.

Interpretability

SHAP integration for feature-level explanations, ensuring clinical transparency and trust in model decisions.

What I'm Working Toward

Research Vision

I believe that subtle biological signals encode profound insights about human health and that the right computational tools can surface them. My goal is to contribute to AI systems that don't just perform well on benchmarks, but that clinicians can trust, explain, and deploy in real diagnostic settings. I'm particularly drawn to the intersection of single-cell genomics, multi-omics integration, and interpretable ML where the complexity of cancer biology meets the precision of modern AI.
